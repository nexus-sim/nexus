## ⚠️ Top Risks

### Risk #1: Demand is Unvalidated (HIGH — 72% likelihood)

8 of 20 informal conversations said "definitely try it" — but zero deposits, zero waitlist, zero financial commitment. At 20 members instead of 35, monthly revenue drops to €4,500 (vs. €9,250 target), consuming 83% of runway.

**→ Fix: Post pre-sale offer (€149/mo for first 50 members, €50 deposit) across Barcelona Digital, Startup Grind BCN, and Poblenou Tech Slack communities. Target 25 commitments by August 2026.**

### Risk #2: Break-Even Requires 87.5% Capacity (HIGH — 68%)

35 members = 87.5% of 40 desks. No new space achieves this. At 25 members (realistic for month 6), revenue = €7,625 vs. €8,500 fixed costs. You're underwater.

**→ Fix: Negotiate rent to €3,500/month (offer 3-year lease). This drops break-even to 25 members.**

### Risk #3: 131 Cafes Within 500m (MEDIUM — 81%)

Brutal competition for coffee walk-ins. Your €3.50-5.50 pricing competes with established neighborhood spots that already have loyal customers.

**→ Fix: Run 2-week coffee pop-up test (€1,000 total cost) in Poblenou. Target €150/day. If you hit it, coffee works. If €75/day — it doesn't. You learn this for €1,000, not €120,000.**

### 🟢 Food & Beverage Expert Agent (Sentiment: +0.25)

**Founder:** *"Can €150/day coffee revenue actually work in Poblenou?"*

**Agent:** "Satan's Coffee Corner does 80+ drinks/day in 45sqm in Born. But Born has 3x the foot traffic of Pallars. In 200sqm in Poblenou, realistic estimate: 25-40 walk-in coffees/day at €4.50 average = €112-180/day. You'll hit €150 on good days but average €120. The real coffee revenue comes from members — 35 members buying 1.5 drinks/day = 52 drinks = €234/day. Combined: €350/day. That's €10,500/month — far better than your €3,500 projection."

**Founder:** *"So the coffee strategy should focus on members, not walk-ins?"*

**Agent:** "Exactly. Walk-ins are bonus. Your coffee bar exists to (a) reduce member churn because they love the daily ritual, and (b) attract walk-ins who later convert to members. It's a funnel, not a standalone revenue stream."

## 🔄 The What-If That Changed Everything

After reading the report, the founder asked:

> *"What if I launch coffee-only first with €45K investment in a 70sqm space at €2,200/month rent, opening August 2026? Maria runs coffee full-time. I keep my freelance income (€2K/month). No co-working desks until coffee proves the location works (target: €150/day for 3 consecutive months). Then expand to co-working in February 2027 with remaining €75K."*

### Result: Sentiment shifted from -0.05 → +0.35 (▲ 0.40)

The entire simulation re-evaluated. Leaders who were skeptical flipped positive. The verdict changed from **HIGH RISK** to **PROCEED WITH PHASE 1.**

Why the shift:
- **Capital at risk dropped 62%** — from €120K to €45K
- **Validation is built into the plan** — coffee must work before co-working launches
- **Personal income preserved** — freelance €2K/month covers living costs during validation
- **Clear go/no-go criteria** — €150/day for 3 months = expand. Below that = pivot or stop.
- **Maria's skills are fully utilized** — she runs the coffee bar (her strength) without co-working complexity

**Updated recommendation:** Launch Phase 1 coffee bar in August 2026. If €150/day revenue achieved for 3 consecutive months → commit remaining €75K to co-working expansion in February 2027. If not → you've spent €45K to learn the answer, not €120K.
